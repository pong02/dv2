Minimum wage, BigMac and Happiness

South East Asia, 2020

created by Pong Zhong Zhe

Minimum hourly wage vs BigMac price

The first thing we can notice from this chart is that Singapore has a minimum wage of 0. This is actually because Singapore has no minimum wage regulations.

Besides, it is also noticeable that BigMac prices do not scale to the minimum wage, and even stays prices at around 2-4 USD converted.

This almost consistent nature of an international franchised product will come in handy later.

Countries and minimum hours worked for 1 BigMac

The average hours to earn enough for 1 BigMac is around 2 hours, with India and Pakistan being the biggest outlier with 7 and 4 hours respectively.

This observation above tells us that the price of BigMacs and the minimum wage are NOT the only factor that affects the Happiness Score

of the country as Pakistan and India has very similar wages and BigMac prices but have very different Happiness Scores.

BigMacs affordable after 8 hour shift

As mentioned above, we can indeed see that India and Pakistan has the lowest amount of BigMac affordable with an 8 hour shift worked.

The most BigMacs affordable is held by South Koreans, who only has to work 24 minutes to earn enough for a BigMac!

Approximated valuation of local currency against USD

These 2 charts are actually a basic view on The Economist's Big Mac Index, which is invented as a layman's way to approximate currency valuation based

off of purchasing power parity between countries, which uses BigMac as the "identical basket of goods".

Here, we can see that Singapore has the least undervalued currency whereas Malaysia and India has the highest undervaluation at 51%.

This however, is to be taken with a pinch of salt as a BigMac is not the ideal baseline due to business and operation overheads

that are not factored into the calculations.
